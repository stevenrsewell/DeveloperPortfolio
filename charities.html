Organizations That I Support

I support these charities because I align with their missions and values. These organizations are committed to making a positive impact on issues that matter to me, and I believe in the work they do to bring about meaningful change in the world.

Hack The Hood

During my time as an employee at LegalZoom, the company offered for all full-time employees to choose an approved 501(c)3 non-profit, and LegalZoom would make a monetary donation to that organization in the employee's name. The organization that I chose was Hack The Hood, a non-profit organization based in Oakland, California. Hack The Hood provides tech training, job opportunities, and entrepreneurship skills for low-income and underrepresented youth in the tech industry.

www.hackthehood.org/

Ryan Callahan Foundation

www.ryancallahanfoundation.org

Ryan Callahan is a former NHL player who played 13 seasons with the New York Rangers and later the Tampa Bay Lightning. Throughout his career, Callahan was known for his strong work ethic, on-ice leadership, willingness to block shots, and his ability to contribute both offensively and defensively, earning him a captaincy with the Rangers. He also represented the United States in Olympic and International Play. Callahan and his wife, Kyla founded the Ryan Callahan Foundation, which is dedicated to making a positive impact on the lives of newly diagnosed pediatric cancer patients and their families.

Shiba Inu Rescues

In 2014 I adopted a Shiba Inu named Kuma. Shibas are known for their fox-like appearance, spirited personalities, and strong sense of loyalty to their owners. There are several Shiba Inu rescue organizations across the United States, which focus on fostering, rehoming, and provding care for Shiba Inus to make them adoption-ready for their forever family. Many of these organizations are a network of volunteers who donate their time and energy to help transport Shibas to supportive families.
